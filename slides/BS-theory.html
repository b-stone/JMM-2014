Boij-Soederberg Theory

Let $S = \Bbbk[x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_d]$ (standard graded $\Bbbk$ algebra over a field $\Bbbk$). The Betti diagram of an $S$-module $M$ is the table

$$\beta(M) = \begin{pmatrix} \beta_{0,0}(M) & \beta_{1,1}(M) & \cdots & \beta_{i,i}(M) & \cdots & \beta_{n,n}(M) \\ \beta_{0,1}(M) & \beta_{1,2}(M) & \cdots & \beta_{i,i+1}(M) & \cdots & \beta_{n,n+1}(M) \\ \vdots & \vdots && \vdots && \vdots \\ \beta_{0,j}(M) & \beta_{1,1+j}(M) & \cdots & {\beta_{i,i+j}(M)} & \cdots & \beta_{j,n+j}(M) \\ \vdots & \vdots && \vdots && \vdots \end{pmatrix}.$$

where $\beta_{i,j}(M)$ is the number of minimal degree $j$ generators of $\text{Syz}_{i}(M)$.

Everyday I'm Shuffling

Let ${\bf c} = (0,3,5)$ and ${\bf d} = (0,1,6)$ be degree sequences. Take first differences: $\Delta {\bf c} = (3,2)$, $\Delta {\bf d} = (1,5)$. The order-preserving permutations (or shuffles) of $\Delta {\bf c}$ and $\Delta {\bf d}$ are \[ (\alert{3},\alert{2},1,5),\, (\alert{3},1,\alert{2},5), \, (\alert{3},1,5,\alert{2}), \, \, (1,\alert{3},\alert{2},5), \,(1,\alert{3},5,\alert{2}), \text{ and } (1,5,\alert{3},\alert{2}). \] Treat each shuffle as the first difference of a degree sequence starting with $c_0 + d_0 = 0$: \[\Sigma((3,2,1,5),c_0+d_0) = (0,3,5,6,11).\] \begin{align*} \text{Then } \pi({\bf c})\cdot\pi({\bf d}) & = \,\,\, \pi(0,\alert{3},\alert{5},6,11) \\ &\ \ + \pi(0,\alert{3},4,\alert{6},11) \\ &\ \ + \pi(0,\alert{3},4,9,\alert{11}) \\ &\ \ + \pi(0,1,\alert{4},\alert{6},11) \\ &\ \ + \pi(0,1,\alert{4},9,\alert{11}) \\ &\ \ + \pi(0,1,6,\alert{9},\alert{11}). \end{align*}
